Development of a Driver Assistant and
Vehicle Sensory System with Vehicle Fine Management

Our Project

This research project focuses on the creation of a comprehensive Driver Assistant and Vehicle Sensory System (DAVSS) integrated with cutting-edge Vehicle Fine Management capabilities with the goal of enhancing road safety, optimizing vehicle performance, and improving the overall driving experience

RESEARCH PROBLEM

In what ways can the efficiency of the overall Sri Lankan traffic management system be increased with the implementation of an automated vehicle fine management system, with the additions of a driver assistant and vehicle sensory system?





Key Components




Detect ROAD SIGN BOARDS USING A CAMERA AND PROVIDE APPROPRIATE INSTRUCTIONS AND ALERTS TO THE DRIVER

To accurately detect road sign boards using a camera and classify the type of road sign, a combination of computer vision and machine learning technologies can be employed. A camera mounted on the vehicle captures real-time footage of the road, and the system processes this video feed to identify road signs. Deep learning models can be trained to recognize common road sign shapes, colors, and symbols. Once a road sign is detected and classified, the system can send this information to a mobile app connected to the vehicle. The mobile app can then provide appropriate instructions and alerts to the driver, such as speed limit reminders, yield warnings, or guidance on upcoming road conditions. This integrated solution enhances road safety and driver awareness, contributing to a safer and more informed driving experience.

Detect driver sleepiness and eye health issues during prolonged driving and provide suggestions

Using an HD camera connected to a system, the detection of driver sleepiness can be achieved by monitoring the blink rate. The camera continuously captures the driver's face, and machine learning models, trained to recognize open and closed eyes, can process the video feed. Google Vision libraries can be utilized to facilitate this eye state classification. The system evaluates the effectiveness of these models in real-time by analyzing the blink rate patterns and comparing them to established thresholds for alertness. Additionally, this technology can predict potential eye health issues based on the blink rate data, identifying irregularities that may indicate eye strain or other conditions.

Detect air quality outside of the vehicle using sensors and provide appropriate instruction of the relevant health risk to driver

The primary aim of this research is to advance road safety by creating an external air quality monitoring system designed for vehicles. Utilizing MQ gas sensors in conjunction with Raspberry Pi technology, this system is engineered to deliver up-to-the-minute data on air quality to drivers. Its purpose is to empower drivers with crucial information regarding air pollution, enabling them to receive real-time health risk alerts and make informed choices that protect their well-being during their journeys. By providing this innovative tool, the research strives to enhance road safety and contribute to a healthier and more informed driving experience for individuals on the road.

FINE MANAGEMENT AND CHATBOT

To expedite the processing of fines and enhance system efficiency, several key strategies can be implemented. Firstly, streamlining administrative processes through digitalization and automation can significantly reduce processing times. Automation of data entry, integration with databases, and real-time payment processing can expedite fine handling. Additionally, optimizing system resources, such as server capacity and network bandwidth, can mitigate delays during periods of high demand. User feedback and experience can be instrumental in identifying bottlenecks and areas for improvement, leading to a more user-friendly and efficient system. Regular user testing and feedback collection are essential for ongoing enhancements to ensure an efficient and responsive fine processing system.
